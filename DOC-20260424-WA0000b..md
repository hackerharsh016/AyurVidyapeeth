SROTAS SHARIR

**INTRODUCTION –** Classical ayurvedic texts proclaim *Srotomayam* *Hi* *Shariram* which means our body is a complex system of enumerable channels which are designed for various physiological activities of the body. They are responsible for formation transformation and transportation of all *Dosha-Dhatu-Mala* inthebody*.* On comparison with modern physiological systems the term *Srotas* can be closely relate with networks of circulatory system, lymphatic channels, neuronal pathways, channels of interstitial fluid and cellular communication. The concept of *Srotas* is very important for *Ayurvedic* *Vaidyas,* as it is the key concept from both physiological and pathological view. *Dosha Dhatu* and *Mala* attain their *Prakrut* or *Vikrut* conditions in this channels and treatment also depends on the *Dushit Srotas. Aacharya Sushruta* has also mentioned about *Srotovidhha lakshan* (traumatic symptoms) from surgical point of view.

**So let’s get ready to learn such a important concept of Ayurveda with SROTOVEDA !!**

**ORIGIN (UTPATTI) OF SROTAS –**

**यथार्थमूष्मणा युक्तोवायुः स्रोतांसि दारयेत् । (सु. शा. ४/२८)**

*Srotas* are originated due to combination of *Vata* and *Pitta Dosha.*

**DEFINITION OF SROTAS –**

**स्रोतांसि खलु परिणाममापद्यमानानां धातूनामभिवाहीनि भवन्त्ययनार्थेन। ( च. वि. ५/१३)**

After the formation of *Ahara rasa, the* transformationof its *Dhatu Poshakansh* into *Sthayi Dhatu is necessary*. The channels through which the transform,ation and transportation of such *substances occur are known as Srotas.*

*मूलात् खादन्तर देहे प्रसृतं त्वभिवाहि यत् ।*

**स्रोतस तदिति विज्ञेयं सिरा धमनी वर्जितम् ।। ( सु. ना. ९/१३ )**

Srotas are the hollow spaces (*Akash* *Mahabhuta* *Yukta*) which are responsible for transportation of all physiological entities (*Bhav Padarth)* in the body except *Sira* and *Dhamni.*

**1.** *Srotas* take part in the transformation of *Dhatus* ie formation and transportation of various body substances.

3. *Srotas* are gutter like hollow spaces.

4. *Srotas* secrete *Rasa*.

***Udakavahe dwe –*** The term *Udaka* here, is not directed related with normal drinking water. It can be correlated with Lymph.

This fluid also accumulate in the abdomen in the peritoneum known as a *Dakodar* (peritoneal fluid.

परिपाकात् परिणामेन दकोदरता सर्वेषामेव; अन्यानि उदराणि परिपाकादुदकभावं भजन्ते, दकोदरं प्रागेवेति विशेषतः । (डल्हण निदान अध्याय ७ )

The *Udakavaha Srotas* are mostly involved in abdominal disorders, and for them the terms *Ambuvahi* or *Jalavaha* are also used.

In *Udar Roga*, there is dysfunction of the lymphatic channels (*Lasika Vahini*).

The context of *Udakavaha Srotas* is appropriate to specifically correlate with left and right *Rasakulya*, i.e., the Thoracic duct and the Right lymphatic duct.

If Lymphatic duct (*Rasakulya* ) and lymphatic channels (*Lasikavahini*) are considered to be Udakavaha Srotas, then their root *Mula Sthana* or *Prabhava Sthana* ( site of origination becomes the entire body, rather than a single specific location.

**To justify *Talu* (palate) as a *Mula Sthana* :**

The fluid present in the abdomen (*Udaragata Jala*) is related to the body fluids (*Shariragata* *Jala*), and thirst (*Trusha*) is also related to the body fluids. Both these facts are undisputed.

In Udar Roga, due to the vitiation (*Dushti*) of the *Ambuvaha Srotas*, fluid accumulates in the abdominal cavity (*Udarguha*).

In Western medicine, although thirst (*Pipasa*) is not mentioned among the symptoms of *Jalodara*, it may still arise at the time when *Jalodara* develops.

As *Udargata Jala* has correlation with *Sharirgata Jala* ( Body fluids ) Thirst could be an obvious Symptom of *Jalodar*.

A human experiences the sensation of thirst only in the region around the throat, not elsewhere. Therefore, at the time of thirst, references are always made to the throat, palate, and other parts of the mouth.

The *Udakavaha Srotas* are related to water; water is related to thirst; thirst is related to the throat; and the throat is related to the palate.

Based on this line of reasoning, the *Mula Sthana* (root site) of the *Udakavaha Srotas* is considered to be the palate (*Talu*).

*Pipasa –*

According to Western scholars, the origin of thirst is explained as follows: when the water content in the body decreases, the secretion of saliva and other glands in the mouth stops. As a result, the palate, throat, and related parts become dry, and this dryness leads to the sensation of thirst.

However, from the direct anatomical point of view, there is no apparent relationship between the palate (*Talu*) and the *Udakavaha Srotas.*

*Sadyomarnam –* Here, the *Udakavaha Srotas* situated in the abdomen are intended; therefore, any trauma to the abdomen may result in rapid death.

* **Dr. B.G. Ghanekar – ( on Sushruta Samhita ) –**

*Rasavaha Srotas* should be correlated with the small channels that carry ‘*Rasa’* throughout the body, which in modern terminology are called capillaries.

The *Pranavaha Srotas* is also said to have the same root location (*Mula Sthana*) as the *Rasavaha Srotas*, but according to modern understanding, a distinction has been made between the two.

Blood circulation in the body is divided into two parts.

1. *Laghu Paribhraman* (Lesser circulation)
2. *Deergha Paribhraman* (Greater circulation)

In these, the lesser (pulmonary) circulation is related to the transport of respiratory air (*Pranavayu*) and occurs in the lungs, whereas the greater (systemic) circulation is related to the nourishment of the entire body and takes place in parts other than the lungs. Therefore, *Pranavaha Srotas* can be understood as pulmonary capillaries and *Rasavaha Srotas* as systemic capillaries.

Since both have the heart (*Hridaya*) as their root, their symptoms of injury are similar, and damage to these vital points (*Marma*) can lead to death.

**RAKTAVAHA SROTAS**

**Photo**

*Raktavaha Srotas* are the *Ayurvedic* channels responsible for the production, circulation, and transport of *Rakta Dhatu* (blood).

***Mula Sthana –***

* ***Charak –*** *Yakrut* and *Pliha*
* ***Sushruta –*** *Yakrut, Pliha* and *Raktvahi Dhamniya*

**Description by *Aacharya Sushruta –***

**रक्तवहे द्वे तयोर्मुलं यकृतप्लीहानौ रक्तवाहिन्यश्च धमन्यः।**

**तत्र विद्धस्य‌ श्यावङ्गता ज्वरों दाहः पाण्डुता शोणितागमनं रक्तनेत्रता च।।**

There are two *Raktavaha Srotas.*

*Yakrut, Pliha* and *Raktvahi Dhamniya.*

***Vidhha Lakshan* ( Traumatic Causes ) *–***

1. *Shyavangta –* Bluish/blackish discoloration of the body
2. *Jwaro –* Fever
3. *Daha -* Burning sensation
4. *Panduta –* Pallor (paleness)
5. *Shonitagamanam –* Bleeding
6. *Raktanetrata –* Redness of the eyes

**Description by *Aacharya Charak –***

**शोणितवहानां स्रोतसां यकृन्मूलं प्लीहा च|**

***Mula Sthana :*** *Yakrut* and *Pliha*

***Dushti Lakshan*** *:* The *Dushti Lakshan* of *Raktavaha Srotas* is similar to the *Rakta dhatu Dushti Lakshan.*

***Dushti Hetu :***

**विदाहीन्यन्नपानानि स्निग्धोष्णानि द्रवाणि च|**

**रक्तवाहीनि दुष्यन्ति भजतां चातपानलौ||**

1. *Vidahi Annapanani –*Foods and drinks that cause burning or irritation
2. *Snigdha –* Oily or unctuous substances
3. *Ushnani –* Hot (in temperature or potency) substances
4. *Dravani –* Liquid substances
5. *Aatap sevan -* Exposure to sunlight
6. *Agnisevan –* Exposure to fire or heat

**Key points by different commentators –**

* **Dr. B.G. Ghanekar – ( on Sushruta Samhita ) –**

*Raktavaha Srotas* should be correlated with the capillaries present in the liver and spleen.

According to modern understanding, the blood circulation of the liver (portal circulation) is considered independent, and accordingly, *Raktavaha Srotas* can be correlated with portal capillaries.

When the liver (*Yakrut*) and spleen (*Pliha*) are injured, there is a risk of their rupture.

If rupture occurs, death usually happens; however, if the injury is not very severe, death may not occur.

***Shyavangata*** – Discoloration of the body (change in complexion).

***Daha*** (burning) – This is an indication/symptom of pain.

***Shonitagamanam*** – This bleeding does not occur outside the body, but happens internally within the liver, spleen, or inside the abdominal cavity.

***Raktanetrata*** – The term “*Rakta*” should be understood more as “*discoloration*” rather than strictly redness.

This yellowish discoloration is the result of jaundice produced during the breakdown/absorption of blood within the body.

Symptoms of liver and spleen injury (according to modern science):

Shock, pain, tenderness, blood loss, some degree of jaundice, and well-marked fever (pyrexia) with initial shock.

Even if we do not consider direct injury (*Vedha*), but follow *Charaka Samhita* and consider the symptoms of vitiation (*Dushti Lakshana*), these symptoms resemble those seen in diseases affecting the liver and spleen, such as:

*Kalajar* (chronic fever conditions)

*Vishama Jwara* (irregular fever)

Various *Pandu Roga* (types of anemia)

*Kamala* (jaundice)

**MAMSAVAHA SROTAS**

**Photo**

*Mamsavaha Srotas* are the *Ayurvedic* transport channels responsible for carrying nutrients to and nourishing muscle tissue (*Mamsa Dhatu*).

***Mula Sthana –***

* ***Charak –*** *Snayu* and *Twak*
* ***Sushruta –*** *Snayu, Twacha,* and *Raktavahi Dhamniya*

**Description by *Aacharya Sushruta* –**

**मांसवहे द्वे तयोर्मुलं स्नायुत्वचं रक्तवाहश्च धमन्यः ।**

**तत्र विद्धस्य‌ श्र्वथुमसि शोषः सिरा ग्रंथियों मरणं च ||**

There are two *Mamsavaha Srotas.*

*Snayu, Twacha,* and *Raktavahi Dhamniya.*

***Vidhha Lakshan* ( Traumatic Causes ) –**

1. *Shwayathu –* Swelling (inflammation)
2. *Mamsi Shoshah –* Wasting or atrophy of muscles
3. *Sira Granthayo –* Hardness/thickening of veins
4. *Marnam –* Death

**Description by *Aacharya Charak –***

**मांसवहानां च स्रोतसां स्नायुर्मूलं त्वक् च|**

***Mula Sthana :*** *Snayu* and *Twak*

***Dushti Lakshan :*** The *Dushti Lakshan* of *Mamsavaha Srotas* is similar to the *Mamsa dhatu Dushti Lakshan*.

***Dushti Hetu :***

**अभिष्यन्दीनि भोज्यानि स्थूलानि च गुरूणि च| मांसवाहीनि दुष्यन्ति भुक्त्वा च स्वपतां दिवा||**

1. *Abhishyandini bhojyani –* Intake of *abhishyandi* substances (foods that cause obstruction/blockage in body channels)
2. *Sthulani –* Intake of dense/gross *(sthula)* substances
3. *Guruni –* Intake of heavy *(guru)* substances
4. *Bhuktwa cha Swaptam diva –* sleeping during the daytime after meals.

**Key points by different commentators –**

* **Dr. B.G. Ghanekar – ( on Sushruta Samhita ) –**

The *Māṃsavaha Srotas* can be understood as the muscular capillaries present within the muscle tissue.

The reason its root (*mūla*) is considered to be the skin (*twacha*) and ligaments/tendons (*snāyu*) is that signs of injury to the skin and ligaments are reflected in this system.

A minor injury to the skin does not cause death, but an injury to the skin in regions like the veins (*śirā*) and neck (*grīvā*), and if that wound becomes infected, can lead to subcutaneous inflammation (cellulitis), which may ultimately result in death.

**MEDOVAHA SROTAS**

**Photo**

Ayurvedic channels responsible for transporting and metabolizing adipose tissue (*Meda* *Dhatu*).

***Mula Sthana***

* ***Charak –*** *Vrikka* and *Vapavahan*
* ***Sushruta –*** *Kati* and *Vrikka*

**Description by *Aacharya Sushruta –***

**मेदोवहे द्वे तयोर्मुलं कटी वृक्कौ च ,**

**तत्र विद्धस्य‌ स्वेदापगमनं स्निग्धांङ्गता तालुशोषः स्थूलशोफता पिपासा च ।।**

There are two *Medovaha Srotas.*

Kati and Vrikka

***Vidhha Lakshan* ( Traumatic Causes ) *–***

* 1. *Swedapgamanam –* Sweating
  2. *Snigdhangta –* Unctuousness / oiliness of the body parts
  3. *Talus Shoshah –* Dryness of the palate
  4. *Sthula Shofta –* Gross swelling (edema)
  5. *Pipasa –* Thirst

**Description by *Aacharya Charak –***

**मेदोवहानां स्रोतसां वृक्कौ मूलं वपावहनं च|**

***Mula Sthana :*** *Vrikka* and *Vapavahan*

***Dushti Lakshan*** : The *Dushti Lakshan* of *Medovaha Srotas* is similar to the *Meda dhatu Dushti Lakshan.*

***Dushti Hetu :***

**अव्यायामाद्दिवास्वप्नान्मेद्यानां चातिभक्षणात्| मेदोवाहीनि दुष्यन्ति वारुण्याश्चातिसेवनात्||**

1. *Avyayamat –* Lack of exercise / physical inactivity
2. *Divaswapnan –* Sleeping during the day (daytime sleep)
3. *Medyanam cha ati bhakshanaat –* Consumption of meat of fatty/obese animals
4. *Vaarunyascha ati sevanaat –* Excessive alcohol consumption
